## Philosophy & Foundation

### The Four Pillars

VibrationFit's curriculum rests on four foundational principles, adapted for young learners:

| Principle | Adult Language | Child Language | How It Shows Up |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **The basis of life is freedom** | Freedom is the foundation | "I get to choose" | Children choose projects, pace, and expression within structured learning |
| **The purpose of life is joy** | Joy is why we're here | "Learning feels good" | Every subject connects to wonder, play, and delight |
| **The result of life is expansion** | Growth is natural | "I notice I can do more" | Portfolio tracks growth without judgment; celebrates new capacities |
| **Activation through immersion** | Reading/hearing your vision activates it | "When I say it, I feel it" | Daily vision readings, storytelling, and verbal declarations |

### Above the Green Line Learning

In VibrationFit, "Above the Green Line" represents emotional states of joy, appreciation, freedom, curiosity, and love. "Below the Green Line" represents fear, frustration, boredom, and overwhelm.

**The curriculum is designed so children spend the majority of their learning time Above the Green Line.** This means:

- **Curiosity before correction** — Ask "What do you notice?" before "That's wrong."
- **Sensory-rich experiences** — Learning happens through touching, tasting, hearing, seeing, and moving.
- **Voice preservation** — Honor how each child naturally speaks, thinks, and creates. Their voice is sacred.
- **Present-tense celebration** — "You read that word!" not "Soon you'll be a reader."
- **Contrast is information, not failure** — When something doesn't work, that's clarity. "Now I know what I want instead."

### The 5-Phase Learning Rhythm (Adapted from Conscious Creation Flow)

Every lesson or unit follows this natural rhythm:

| Phase | Adult VibrationFit | Child Adaptation | Example (1st Grade Science) |
|-------|-------------------|------------------|----------------------------|
| 1. **Gratitude Opens** | Gratitude opens the creative space | "What do I already love about this?" | "I love watching clouds change shape" |
| 2. **Sensory Detail Amplifies** | Sensory immersion deepens the experience | "Let me touch it, see it, hear it" | Go outside. Lie on grass. Watch clouds. Draw what you see. |
| 3. **Embodiment Stabilizes** | Physical anchoring makes it real | "My body knows this" | Act out being rain: start as a cloud, fall, pool, evaporate |
| 4. **Essence Locks In** | One word captures the feeling | "This makes me feel ___" | "Wonder" or "Huge" or "Free" |
| 5. **Surrender Releases** | Let go of needing to control outcomes | "I did my part, now I let it grow" | Plant a seed. Water it. Walk away. Trust. |

## Florida Compliance Checklist

### Step 1: File Notice of Intent

Send a letter to your county superintendent within 30 days of beginning homeschool. Include:
- Each child's full legal name
- Date of birth
- Home address
- Parent/guardian name

**Note:** File only once. No re-filing annually unless you move counties.

### Step 2: Maintain a Portfolio

Florida law requires a portfolio that includes:
- **Daily log of educational activities** (subjects covered, materials used)
- **Work samples** (writing, math, art, projects, photos)
- **Reading list** (titles and authors of books read/read to the child)

Portfolios must be preserved for **2 years** and presented within 15 days if requested by the school district.

### Step 3: Annual Evaluation

Due on the anniversary of your Notice of Intent filing. Choose one:

| Option | Best For | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| **Portfolio review by FL-certified teacher** | Most families; low stress | Teacher reviews samples, talks with child |
| **Nationally normed achievement test** | Families wanting standardized data | Stanford, Iowa, etc. (available for grade 1+; not applicable for preschool) |
| **State student assessment test** | Alignment with public school benchmarks | Same tests FL public schools use |
| **Psychological evaluation** | Special needs situations | Licensed psychologist required |
| **Other valid measurement** | Creative programs | Requires superintendent approval |

**Recommendation:** Portfolio review is the most natural fit for this curriculum. The daily rhythm and project-based approach generates abundant portfolio material.

### Step 4: Required Subjects

Florida does not mandate specific subjects, but requires **"sequentially progressive instruction"** — meaning each year should build on the previous year in complexity. This curriculum covers all standard subject areas:

- English Language Arts (Reading, Writing, Speaking, Listening)
- Mathematics
- Science
- Social Studies
- Physical Education / Health
- Art / Music / Creative Expression

### Preschool Note

Florida does not require homeschool registration for children under compulsory school age (age 6). The preschool curriculum here is voluntary and designed for enrichment, not compliance. However, it aligns with Florida's VPK (Voluntary Prekindergarten) developmental benchmarks for families who want that alignment.

## The VibrationFit Learning Framework

### 12 Life Categories as Thematic Lenses

VibrationFit organizes life into 12 categories. In this curriculum, these become **thematic lenses** through which academic subjects are explored. Every category naturally contains multiple subjects.

| VF Category | Child Theme | Academic Connections | Essence |
|-------------|------------|---------------------|---------|
| **Fun** | "Things I Love to Do" | PE, Art, Music, Creative Writing | Permission & Lightness |
| **Health** | "My Amazing Body" | Science (Life Science), PE, Nutrition, Health | Energy & Freedom |
| **Travel** | "Places Near & Far" | Social Studies (Geography), Map Skills, Cultural Studies | Wonder & Expansion |
| **Love** | "People I Love" | ELA (Writing about family), Social-Emotional Learning | Connection & Safety |
| **Family** | "My Family Story" | Social Studies (History), ELA (Narrative), Math (Counting family) | Belonging & Joy |
| **Social** | "My Friends & Community" | Social Studies (Civics), SEL, Collaborative Projects | Authenticity & Play |
| **Home** | "Where I Live" | Science (Earth Science), Math (Measurement), Art | Comfort & Peace |
| **Work** | "What I Create" | ELA (How-to Writing), Math (Problem Solving), STEM Projects | Confidence & Pride |
| **Money** | "Counting & Sharing" | Math (Number Sense, Addition, Coins), Economics Concepts | Ease & Generosity |
| **Stuff** | "Things I Use & Make" | Science (Materials/Properties), Art, Math (Sorting/Classifying) | Appreciation & Care |
| **Giving** | "How I Help" | Social Studies (Community), Service Projects, Writing | Overflow & Kindness |
| **Spirituality** | "My Quiet Inside" | Mindfulness, Nature Study, Journaling, Reflection | Trust & Presence |

### The Vibrational Grammar for Children

Just as VibrationFit uses present-tense, positive-framing language for adults, the curriculum teaches children to speak and write this way:

**Language Patterns to Cultivate:**
- "I am..." / "I can..." / "I notice..." / "I enjoy..."
- "I choose..." / "I love when..." / "My favorite part is..."
- Describing what IS, not what isn't
- Sensory-specific language: "It feels warm" instead of "It's nice"

**Language Patterns to Gently Redirect:**
- "I can't" → "I'm still learning how" → eventually "I'm figuring this out"
- "I hate this" → "This isn't my favorite" → "I know what I'd like instead"
- "I'm bored" → "I'm ready for something new" → "I wonder what would feel fun right now"

This is not about suppressing authentic emotion. Below the Green Line feelings are valid and acknowledged. The practice is noticing what you feel, then reaching for a slightly better-feeling thought — the same skill VibrationFit teaches adults through Contrast-to-Clarity.

## First Grade Curriculum

**Aligned with:** Florida B.E.S.T. Standards (ELA & Math), Florida Science Standards (SC.1), Florida Social Studies Standards (SS.1)

### English Language Arts

#### Reading Foundations (FL B.E.S.T. Aligned)

| Standard Area | What It Looks Like in VibrationFit | Portfolio Evidence |
|--------------|-----------------------------------|-------------------|
| **Print Concepts** — title, author, illustrator, glossary | Daily reading with "Who made this book? What did they create?" framing | Photo log of books explored; child identifies parts of a book |
| **Phonological Awareness** — segmenting, blending phonemes | Singing, clapping syllables, rhyming games, sound hunts around the house | Recording of child blending sounds; rhyming word lists child created |
| **Phonics** — digraphs, r-controlled vowels, long vowels, inflectional endings | Phonics through Life Category word walls (Fun words, Health words, Home words, etc.) | Category word wall photos; decodable reading samples |
| **Fluency** — grade-level text with expression; sight word automaticity | Daily "Vision Reading" — child reads aloud from their personal vision or favorite books | Video/audio of child reading; sight word mastery checklist |

**VibrationFit Integration — "My Life I Love" Readers:**
Children create their own simple books using present-tense, first-person sentences about their life. These serve as decodable readers that are personally meaningful:
- "I run in the sun. It is fun." (CVC words + personal truth)
- "My mom reads to me. I like her lap." (sight words + sensory detail)
- "We go to the beach. I see the waves." (category: Travel)

#### Writing (FL B.E.S.T. Aligned)

| Standard Area | VibrationFit Approach | Portfolio Evidence |
|--------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| **Narrative Writing** | "My Best Day" stories using the 5-Phase rhythm (start with gratitude, add sensory detail, embody it, name the feeling, let it be) | Dated writing samples showing progression |
| **Informational Writing** | "What I Know About ___" connected to Life Categories (My body, My home, My pet) | Topic-based writing samples |
| **Opinion Writing** | "I love ___ because ___" — always framed as what they enjoy, not what they dislike | Opinion pieces with reasons |
| **Handwriting** | Practice through Vision Journaling — writing sentences about their life | Handwriting samples across the year |

**VibrationFit Integration — Vision Journaling (age-adapted):**
Three times per week, the child draws a picture and writes 1-3 sentences about something they love about their life. This is their "kid-sized Life Vision" and doubles as writing practice. Present tense. First person. Sensory. Specific.

*Week 1 example:* Drawing of family at dinner. "We eat tacos. I like the crunch. Dad laughs."

#### Speaking & Listening

- **Daily Vision Declaration:** Each morning, child speaks aloud 2-3 sentences about their life using present-tense, positive framing. ("I am a reader. I love my family. My body is strong.")
- **Show & Tell from Life Categories:** Weekly sharing connected to the current category theme.
- **Retelling Practice:** After read-alouds, child retells using "First... Then... And then... My favorite part was..."

### Mathematics

**Aligned with:** Florida B.E.S.T. Math Standards, Grade 1

| Standard Area | VibrationFit Approach | Portfolio Evidence |
|--------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| **Number Sense (to 120)** | Counting real things from Life Categories — coins (Money), heartbeats (Health), steps to the mailbox (Home), seashells (Travel) | Counting logs, number writing samples |
| **Place Value (tens & ones)** | Building with real objects: "10 crayons = 1 bundle. How many bundles in our art box?" | Bundling activities with photos |
| **Addition & Subtraction** | Story problems from real life: "We had 8 strawberries. We ate 3. How many do we enjoy now?" (positive framing) | Math journal with story problems |
| **Comparison** | "Which has more? Which has fewer?" using things they care about | Comparison worksheets and activities |
| **Measurement** | Measuring things at Home — the couch, the garden, how tall the sunflower grew | Measurement log with drawings |
| **Geometry** | Shape hunts around the house and neighborhood; building with blocks | Shape journal, construction photos |
| **Time & Money** | Daily schedule clock practice; coin identification and counting (connects to Money category) | Clock worksheets, coin sorting photos |
| **Data** | Graphing favorites: "Our family's favorite fruits" — surveys and simple bar graphs | Hand-drawn graphs |

**VibrationFit Integration — Abundance Math:**
Math problems are framed in terms of having, sharing, and enjoying — never scarcity or loss. Instead of "You lost 4 apples," use "You shared 4 apples with your friend. How many do you still have to enjoy?"

### Science

**Aligned with:** Florida Science Standards SC.1.E, SC.1.L, SC.1.P, SC.1.N

| Standard Area | Life Category Connection | Activities |
|--------------|-------------------------|------------|
| **Earth & Space** (SC.1.E) — sun, stars, gravity, water, rocks, soil | Home, Travel, Spirituality | Night sky observation journal; sunrise/sunset gratitude practice; rock collection and classification |
| **Life Science** (SC.1.L) — living/nonliving, plant parts, animal needs, heredity | Health, Family, Giving | Plant a family garden (track growth); "My Amazing Body" unit (senses, systems); observe parent/baby animals |
| **Physical Science** (SC.1.P) — push/pull, motion, force | Fun, Stuff | Playground physics (swings = pendulums, slides = inclined planes); build ramps and race cars |
| **Science Practices** (SC.1.N) — observe, question, record | All Categories | Nature journal; "I wonder..." board; simple experiments with observation logs |

**VibrationFit Integration — The 5 Senses as Learning Tools:**
Every science lesson begins with sensory observation. Children are trained to notice: "What do I see? Hear? Feel? Smell?" before analyzing or naming. This mirrors VibrationFit's sensory-rich embodiment practice and builds genuine scientific observation skills.

| **Approaches to Learning** | Fun Category — "I Love to Learn" | Choice-based play stations; project work the child selects; wonder questions ("I wonder what happens if...") |
| **Social-Emotional** | Love/Family/Social — "My Feelings Are My Friends" | Green Line feelings check-in (with color cards); naming emotions; "I feel ___ and that's okay"; cooperative play; turn-taking games |
| **Language & Literacy** | All Categories — "Words Are Magic" | Daily read-aloud (3+ books); storytelling ("Tell me about your picture"); rhyming songs; letter recognition through names and meaningful words; name writing practice |
| **Mathematical Thinking** | Money/Home/Stuff — "Counting My World" | Count everything (steps, crackers, blocks); sorting by color/shape/size; pattern making with real objects; "more/less/same" at snack time |

### Preschool Daily Rhythm

The preschool day is shorter and more fluid than first grade. Everything is play-based.

**Morning Circle (15 min)**
- Good morning song
- Green Line check-in: "How does my body feel right now?" (use feeling cards with faces/colors)
- Vision Declaration (simple): "I am [name]. I love [something]. Today feels [word]."
- Weather + calendar observation

**Exploration Block 1 (45-60 min)**
- Two play stations set up connected to the week's Life Category theme
- Free choice between stations
- Adult plays alongside, asking open-ended questions and modeling language

**Outdoor Time (45-60 min)**
- Unstructured outdoor play
- Nature observation integrated naturally ("Look at that! What do you notice?")
- Gross motor: running, climbing, riding, digging

**Read-Aloud & Snack (20-30 min)**
- 2-3 books connected to the week's theme
- Snack prepared together when possible (math: counting, pouring, measuring)

**Exploration Block 2 (30-45 min)**
- Art, music, or sensory play
- Cooking project
- Building/construction play

**Closing Circle (10 min)**
- "What did I love today?" (Gratitude practice)
- Essence word: "Today felt like ___" (calm, silly, busy, cozy)
- Story or song

### Preschool Skills Progression

Rather than rigid benchmarks, track these **naturally emerging skills** through observation and portfolio:

**Language & Literacy (by end of year):**
- Recognizes own name in print
- Identifies some letters (especially in own name and family names)
- Retells a simple story or event in sequence
- Speaks in complete sentences
- Uses "I" statements to express wants and feelings
- Enjoys being read to; has favorite books

**Math Thinking (by end of year):**
- Counts to 20+ with one-to-one correspondence
- Recognizes numerals 1-10
- Sorts objects by one attribute (color, size, shape)
- Copies and extends simple patterns (AB, ABB)
- Uses comparison language: more, less, same, bigger, smaller

**Science & Inquiry (by end of year):**
- Asks "why" and "how" questions
- Makes observations using senses
- Describes characteristics of living things
- Notices and describes weather changes
- Explores cause and effect through play

**Social-Emotional (by end of year):**
- Names basic emotions in self and others
- Uses words to express needs (instead of only crying/hitting)
- Takes turns in simple games
- Shows empathy toward others
- Separates from caregiver with confidence
- Identifies as part of a family and community

## Daily Rhythm & Weekly Structure

### First Grade Daily Rhythm (Approximately 3-4 hours of focused learning)

The day follows VibrationFit's 5-Phase Conscious Creation rhythm:

**Phase 1: GRATITUDE OPENS — Morning Circle (20-30 min)**
*8:30 - 9:00 AM (adjust to your family)*

- Green Line check-in: "Where am I this morning?" (use a simple 1-5 scale or color)
- Vision Declaration: Child reads or recites 3-5 present-tense sentences about their life
- Gratitude moment: "One thing I love about my life right now is..."
- Preview the day's theme/activities

**Phase 2: SENSORY DETAIL AMPLIFIES — Core Learning Block (60-90 min)**
*9:00 - 10:30 AM*

- Reading / Phonics (30 min) — Hands-on, multi-sensory phonics instruction
- Writing (15-20 min) — Vision Journaling, story writing, or informational writing
- Math (30-40 min) — Manipulatives-based, real-world connected

*Break: Outdoor play, snack, free movement (30 min)*

**Phase 3: EMBODIMENT STABILIZES — Experiential Learning Block (45-60 min)**
*11:00 - 12:00 PM*

- Science, Social Studies, or Project Work (connected to the week's Life Category theme)
- This block is hands-on: experiments, field trips, cooking, building, exploring
- Art and music integrate here naturally

**Phase 4: ESSENCE LOCKS IN — Reflection (10-15 min)**
*12:00 - 12:15 PM*

- "What did I learn today?" — Child shares one thing
- Essence word: "Today's learning felt like ___"
- Add to portfolio: work sample, photo, or journal entry

**Phase 5: SURRENDER RELEASES — Afternoon Freedom**
*After lunch*

- Free play, independent reading, nature time, or rest
- No structured academics — let the morning's learning integrate

### Weekly Structure

| Day | Life Category Focus | Special Activity |
|-----|-------------------|------------------|
| **Monday** | New theme introduction | Library visit or new book selection |
| **Tuesday** | Deep dive — Science/Social Studies connection | Hands-on experiment or project |
| **Wednesday** | Creative expression day | Art, music, drama, or building project |
| **Thursday** | Real-world application | Field trip, cooking, community outing |
| **Friday** | Celebration & portfolio | "Week in Review" — child shares what they loved; add to portfolio |

### Weekly Minimum Hours (For Your Reference)

Florida does not specify minimum hours, but for portfolio confidence:
- **First Grade:** 15-20 hours/week of intentional learning (including read-alouds, projects, field trips, and experiential learning — not just desk work)
- **Preschool:** 8-12 hours/week of structured play and intentional activities (everything else is naturally developmental)

## Yearly Pacing Guide (36 Weeks)

The year is organized into **12 thematic units of 3 weeks each**, one per Life Category. This ensures every subject gets covered while maintaining the VibrationFit framework.

### Unit Pacing

| Unit | Weeks | Life Category | Theme Title | Key Academic Focus |
|------|-------|--------------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1 | 1-3 | **Family** | "My Family Story" | Narrative writing; counting family members; family history; heredity (SC.1.L) |
| 2 | 4-6 | **Home** | "Where I Live" | Informational writing; measurement; Earth's surface & materials (SC.1.E); map skills |
| 3 | 7-9 | **Health** | "My Amazing Body" | Life science — senses, plant/animal needs (SC.1.L); nutrition; addition/subtraction with food |
| 4 | 10-12 | **Fun** | "Things I Love to Do" | Opinion writing ("I love ___ because"); physical science — motion & force (SC.1.P); PE focus |
| 5 | 13-15 | **Social** | "My Friends & Community" | Civics; community helpers; collaborative writing; graphing class/family data |
| 6 | 16-18 | **Money** | "Counting & Sharing" | Coins, addition/subtraction, place value deep dive; economics (wants vs. needs) |
| 7 | 19-21 | **Work** | "What I Create" | How-to/procedural writing; STEM building projects; problem-solving math |
| 8 | 22-24 | **Travel** | "Places Near & Far" | Geography; maps & globes; cultural studies; comparing texts on same topic; Earth & space (SC.1.E — sun, stars) |
| 9 | 25-27 | **Love** | "People Who Love Me" | Poetry & letter writing; Valentine's/kindness focus; social-emotional deep dive |
| 10 | 28-30 | **Stuff** | "Things I Use & Make" | Properties of materials (science); sorting & classifying (math); recycling & care |
| 11 | 31-33 | **Giving** | "How I Help" | Service learning project; persuasive/opinion writing; historical figures of character (SS.1) |
| 12 | 34-36 | **Spirituality** | "My Quiet Inside" | Nature study capstone; reflection & journaling; year-in-review portfolio celebration |

### Within Each 3-Week Unit

**Week 1 — Explore:** Introduce the theme. Read related books. Ask big wonder questions. Begin the unit project.

**Week 2 — Create:** Dive deep into hands-on activities. Science experiments, art projects, writing pieces, math connections. Build, cook, grow, measure.

**Week 3 — Celebrate:** Complete the unit project. Add best work to portfolio. Share with family. Child articulates what they learned and how it felt (Essence word). Create a page for their "Life I Love" book.

## Portfolio & Evaluation Guide

### What to Collect

Your portfolio is your proof of "sequentially progressive instruction." It should show growth across the year.

**Monthly Portfolio Additions (minimum):**

| Category | What to Include | How Many |
|----------|----------------|----------|
| **Reading** | Reading log (titles, dates); audio/video of child reading aloud | Ongoing log + 1 recording/month |
| **Writing** | Vision Journal entries, stories, informational pieces, opinion pieces | 4-6 samples/month |
| **Math** | Math journal pages, problem-solving work, photos of hands-on math | 4-6 samples/month |
| **Science** | Observation logs, experiment records, nature journal drawings | 2-4 samples/month |
| **Social Studies** | Maps, timeline work, community project documentation | 2-3 samples/month |
| **Art/Creative** | Photos of art projects, building projects, performances | 2-4 photos/month |
| **Daily Log** | Brief daily record of subjects/activities covered | Daily (can be a simple checklist) |

### The "Life I Love" Book (Year-Long Portfolio Centerpiece)

Over the course of the year, the child builds a personal book — their first Life Vision document. Each unit adds a page:

- **Unit 1 (Family):** "My family is..." with drawing and sentences
- **Unit 2 (Home):** "My home is..." with floor plan drawing
- **Unit 3 (Health):** "My body can..." with body tracing and labels
- *...and so on through all 12 categories*

By year's end, the child has a 12-page illustrated book of their life, written in present tense, first person — exactly like a VibrationFit Life Vision, but created by a 6-year-old. This is powerful portfolio evidence AND a meaningful personal artifact.

### Preparing for Annual Evaluation (Portfolio Review)

If choosing the certified teacher portfolio review:

1. **Organize by subject** — Tab dividers for ELA, Math, Science, Social Studies, Specials
2. **Show growth** — Include early-year AND late-year samples for comparison
3. **Include the reading log** — Evaluators love seeing volume and variety
4. **Bring the "Life I Love" book** — It demonstrates writing progression, subject integration, and personal engagement
5. **Prepare the child** — Practice having them share what they learned and what they loved
6. **Daily log** — Have your log available showing consistent, varied instruction

### Sample Daily Log Entry

```
Date: October 15, 2026
Theme: Health (My Amazing Body) — Week 2

Reading (30 min): Read "My Five Senses" by Aliki. Practiced digraph /sh/ words.
Writing (15 min): Vision Journal — "I taste the orange. It is sweet and cold. My tongue knows."
Math (30 min): Counted heartbeats before and after jumping jacks. Compared numbers using >, <, =.
Science (45 min): Explored the 5 senses with a mystery bag activity. Recorded observations in science journal.
PE (30 min): Yoga and outdoor play.
Read-aloud (20 min): "The Magic School Bus Inside the Human Body"
```

## Appendix: Green Line Emotional Check-In System (For Both Ages)

### How It Works

VibrationFit's emotional scale, simplified for children:

| Zone | Color | Feeling Words | What We Do |
|------|-------|--------------|------------|
| **Above the Green Line** | Green | Happy, excited, curious, calm, proud, playful, loving | "I notice I feel good. I'm ready to learn/play/create." |
| **On the Green Line** | Yellow | Okay, a little tired, not sure, thinking | "I notice I'm in the middle. What would feel a little better right now?" |
| **Below the Green Line** | Red | Frustrated, sad, angry, scared, overwhelmed | "I notice I feel ___ and that's okay. My feelings are giving me information. I'm going to [take a breath / take a break / ask for help]." |

### Key Principles

1. **No zone is "bad"** — All feelings are information. Below the Green Line tells us what we want instead (Contrast to Clarity).
2. **The goal is awareness, not performance** — Children are never punished for being in the red zone or rewarded for being in the green zone.
3. **Gentle upward reach** — When below the Green Line, we don't jump to joy. We reach for the next slightly better feeling: angry → frustrated → bored → okay → curious → interested → happy.
4. **Model it yourself** — "I notice I'm feeling a little frustrated right now. I'm going to take three deep breaths and that might help me feel better."
