# Books & Media List — Ocean Adventures (Age 6)

All picks are age-appropriate for a 6-year-old. Request them from your library a week ahead (most library apps let you place holds online). Aim for a mix of story books and real-photo nonfiction each week.

---

## Week 1 — Meeting the Ocean

| Book | Author | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| *Commotion in the Ocean* | Giles Andreae | Rhyming tour of ocean animals; great read-aloud rhythm |
| *Ocean! Waves for All* | Stacy McAnulty | The ocean tells its own funny story; big-picture "why the ocean matters" |
| *Swimmy* | Leo Lionni | Classic; problem-solving and teamwork (ties to SEL goals) |
| *Hello Ocean* | Pam Muñoz Ryan | The beach through all five senses |

## Week 2 — Ocean Animals & Habitats

| Book | Author | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| *Over in the Ocean: In a Coral Reef* | Marianne Berkes | Counting song format — literacy + math in one |
| *Mister Seahorse* | Eric Carle | Habitats and camouflage flaps |
| *A House for Hermit Crab* | Eric Carle | Habitat concept + months of the year |
| *National Geographic Kids: Ocean Animals* (any level 1–2 reader) | Nat Geo Kids | Real photographs; early-reader text |
| *Clark the Shark* | Bruce Hale | Fun shark story with an SEL message (self-control) |

## Week 3 — Young Researcher Week

| Book | Author | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| *The Big Book of the Blue* | Yuval Zommer | Browsable nonfiction; perfect for answering Wonder Wall questions |
| *Shark Lady* | Jess Keating | True story of Eugenie Clark — who founded Mote Marine Laboratory! Read before/after the visit |
| *If Sharks Disappeared* | Lily Williams | Why the ocean needs every animal (food webs, conservation) |
| *Manfish: A Story of Jacques Cousteau* | Jennifer Berne | A curious boy who became a famous ocean explorer |
| *Coral Reefs* | Gail Gibbons | Clear, kid-level nonfiction on reefs |

## Week 4 — The Deep Sea & Celebration

| Book | Author | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| *Giant Squid* | Candace Fleming | Mysterious and dramatic — answers "what lives at the very bottom?" |
| *The Rainbow Fish* | Marcus Pfister | Classic; sharing and friendship |
| *Somewhere in the Ocean* | Jennifer Ward | Counting rhyme with ocean animals |
| *Down Down Down: A Journey to the Bottom of the Sea* | Steve Jenkins | Travels from surface to trench — mind-blowing for this age |
