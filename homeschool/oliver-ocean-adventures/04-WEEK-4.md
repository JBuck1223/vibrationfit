# Week 4: Putting It All Together — Vowel Teams, Time, Money & Shapes

**Phonics Focus:** Vowel teams **ee, ea, ai, ay** (s**ee**, d**ee**p, r**ee**f, s**ea**, b**ea**ch, s**ai**l, b**ai**t, spr**ay**, d**ay**) + **compound words** (star+fish, sea+weed, jelly+fish, sea+shell, sail+boat, tide+pool, sand+castle)
**Sight Words:** review all 15 from the month
**Math Focus:** Time (analog & digital, AM/PM), money (coins and paying), and geometry (polygons!) — one per day
**Big Idea:** Show how much he's grown: Drawing #2, the Wonder Wall finale, and the Ocean Museum celebration.

**Daily spine:** calendar + date (he should be writing it independently now), counting warm-up (race up the 120 chart), 10 min reading, journal sentence.

## Books This Week

*Giant Squid* (what's at the very bottom?), *Down Down Down* (surface to trench), *The Rainbow Fish*, *Somewhere in the Ocean* — plus HIS favorite of the month, reread.
The titles alone are a vowel-team hunt: s**ea**, d**ee**p, oc**ea**n...

## Monday — Vowel Teams + Clock Day

**Phonics: When Two Vowels Team Up**
"ee and ea both say /ē/ — the ocean is FULL of them." Word hunt: sea, see, deep, reef, beach, seal, eat, seat, wee, feet. Then ai/ay: sail, bait, wait, rain, day, spray, bay.
- Sort word cards by team; read them; dictation: "Write *deep*. Write *sail*."
- Find five vowel-team words in tonight's read-aloud.

**Math: Ocean Clock Day**
- Review the clock face: hour hand (short, slow like a sea turtle), minute hand (long, fast like a sailfish).
- Practice **hours and half-hours** on an analog clock AND digital: "Mote class was at 1:30 — show me." "Snack at 3:00 — show me."
- **AM/PM sort:** make cards of his real day (wake up, breakfast, school time, Mote class, dinner, bedtime) and sort into AM vs PM. "Is 7 AM or 7 PM when you brush your teeth? Both!"
- All week: "What time is it?" at natural moments — hour and half-hour only.

---

## Tuesday — Compound Words + The Aquarium Gift Shop

**Phonics: Compound Word Puzzles**
The ocean invented compound words: **star+fish, sea+shell, sea+weed, jelly+fish, sail+boat, tide+pool, sand+castle, sea+horse.** Write each half on separate cards, scatter them, and he assembles the puzzles. Then the reverse: "What two words hide inside *starfish*?" He picks two and writes them in the journal.

**Math: The Aquarium Gift Shop (money)**
Set up a shop: price shells and toys at 5¢, 10¢, 25¢, 40¢... He's the customer first, then the shopkeeper (making change comes later — for now, **paying the exact amount owed**):
- Coin check: penny 1¢, nickel 5¢, dime 10¢, quarter 25¢
- "The shell costs 30¢ — pay me." (three dimes? a quarter and a nickel? Both! Count each way.)
- Shopkeeper round: YOU pay, he counts whether it's right — catch him with a wrong amount once
- Challenge: "You have 50¢. Can you afford the 35¢ starfish AND the 20¢ shell?"

---

## Wednesday — Drawing #2 + Polygon Hunt

**Creative Time: Second Ocean Drawing**
He draws the ocean again with everything he now knows. Then bring out **Drawing #1** and compare side by side: "What did you add? What did you learn that you didn't know before?" This comparison is the heart of the unit — he sees his own growth. He writes **three sentences** about the new drawing (Week 1 was one sentence — that's growth on paper). Date and save both.

**Math: Polygon Hunt (geometry)**
- Name the shape family: triangle (3 sides), rectangle, square, **pentagon (5), hexagon (6), octagon (8), rhombus, trapezoid**. "An OCTagon has 8 sides like an OCTopus has 8 arms" — he'll never forget it.
- Hunt shapes in his drawing: "The fish body is an oval, the tail is a triangle, the treasure chest is a rectangle."
- Build a fish/boat/crab from pattern blocks or cut paper shapes; count sides and corners (vertices) on each.
- 3D bonus with the recycling bin: sphere (ball), cube (box), cylinder (tube), cone.

## Thursday — Wonder Wall Finale + Re-Assessment

**Inquiry: Read the whole Wonder Wall together.**
"Look how much you knew before we started — and look what you DISCOVERED." Which questions did we answer? Which are still mysteries? (Unanswered ones seed next month — send them to the curriculum writer.)

**Re-assessment (mirrors Week 1's placement, disguised as games):**
- Phonics ladder re-run: CVC → digraphs → magic e → vowel teams. Where's his rung now vs. Week 1?
- Reads a decodable page aloud — smoother than Week 1?
- Counting: race up the 120 chart; count on from 35; backward from 30; skip count by 2s, 5s, 10s
- Facts: five quick fish-bowl problems (sums/differences within 20, one missing-number)
- "Show me 1:30." "Pay me 45¢." "Point to the hexagon."
- He writes the date and one sentence unassisted

Record it all next to Week 1's baseline — that side-by-side IS your portfolio evidence of progress.

## Friday — Ocean Celebration Day

**The Ocean Museum:** Oliver hosts — displays both drawings, his Scientist Journal, crafts, tally charts, and the Wonder Wall, and gives the family a guided tour. He's presenting a month of learning orally, in complete sentences, to a real audience.

**Movement:** ocean dance party — all three videos, freeze-dance finale.

**Story Time:** his favorite of the month. He retells it: who, what happened first/next/last, and his favorite part.

**Snack:** his pick of the month's snacks — he "buys" the ingredients from your pantry shop with coins first.

**Celebrate the process:** "You explained your thinking." "You noticed details." "Your questions made this month amazing."
