# Week 4: Putting It All Together

**Focus Letter:** N (uppercase + lowercase, sound /n/ as in Narwhal, Net)
**Goal Word:** OCEAN — built, traced, copied, and written from memory
**Vocabulary:** Review all words from the month; add any he's collected
**Big Idea:** Complete the word, create Drawing #2, compare it to Week 1, and celebrate how much he's learned.

## Monday — Letter N Completes the Word

**Letter Time:** Introduce N. "N says /n/ — Narwhal! Net! Night!" Trace, punch-trace, Play-Doh. Then the big moment: add N to O-C-E-A. **The word is complete: OCEAN.**

Practice the full sequence from the curriculum:
1. **Say it** — point to each letter, say its name and sound
2. **Build it** — magnetic letters, tiles, or Play-Doh
3. **Trace it** — finger first, then pencil
4. **Write it** — copy onto paper

As he builds, ask: "What letter is this? What sound does it make? What comes next?"

**Math:** Ordinal warm-up using the word — "Which letter is FIRST? Which is LAST? Which letter is THIRD?"

---

## Tuesday — Ocean Letter Hunt

**Letter Time / Movement combined: Ocean Letter Hunt**
Hide the five letters O, C, E, A, N around the room (or yard). As Oliver finds each letter, he places it in the correct order to build OCEAN. Add challenges:
- Swim like a fish to find the next letter
- Crab-walk back with each letter
- Say the letter's sound before it "counts"

Play multiple rounds — scramble the hiding spots, race the timer.

**Math Through Play:** Pattern review with a twist — make patterns with the letter cards themselves (O-C-O-C..., O-C-E-O-C-E...). "What comes next?"

**CVC Word Bonus (for a confident day):** Using blocks or tiles, build simple 3-letter words with this month's letters and sounds: can, cap, net, ten, on, an. Only if he's enjoying it — no pressure.

---

## Wednesday — Drawing #2 + The Big Comparison

**Letter Time:** Write OCEAN on the whiteboard from memory (or copying — wherever he is, celebrate it).

**Creative Time: Second Ocean Drawing**
Invite Oliver to draw the ocean again — everything he now knows. Same open-ended questions as Week 1: "Tell me about your picture. Who lives here? What's happening under the water?"

**Then bring out Drawing #1 from Week 1 and compare side by side.** Ask:
- What did you add this time?
- What did you learn that you didn't know before?
- What's your favorite thing you learned this month?

This comparison is the heart of the whole unit — he SEES his own growth. Write his words on the back, date it, and display both drawings together.

**Extend:** Label parts of the new drawing (ocean, fish, crab, shell, wave, reef). Count the animals — are there more than in Drawing #1? Sort them: fins vs. shells, mammals vs. egg-layers.

---

## Thursday — Revisit the Wonder Wall + Assessment Through Play

**Letter Time:** Speed round — he writes each letter O, C, E, A, N as you call out its SOUND (not its name). Then writes OCEAN one more time.

**Inquiry: Wonder Wall Finale**
Read through the whole Wonder Wall together:
- "What Oliver Knows" (Week 1): "Look how much you knew before we even started!"
- "What Oliver Is Wondering": Which questions did we answer? Which are still mysteries? (Unanswered ones can seed next month's learning — tell the curriculum writer!)

**Gentle re-assessment (mirrors Week 1, disguised as games):**
- Letter hunt: point to letters (focus + any tricky ones from Week 1's assessment) — can he name them and their sounds?
- Count 10 objects forward and backward
- Show an AB pattern — can he continue it and explain the rule?
- Ask him to write OCEAN and any other words he wants to try

Note wins and still-growing skills for next month's plan.

---

## Friday — Ocean Celebration Day

Make it a party. He's earned it.

**Museum Show:** Oliver hosts an "Ocean Museum" — displays his two drawings, crafts, Scientist Journal, and the Wonder Wall. Family members visit and HE gives the tour, explaining everything. (Confidence sharing ideas: final boss level.)

**Movement:** Ocean dance party — all three movement videos, plus freeze dance.

**Story Time:** His favorite book of the month, read by him "reading" the pictures, or a final story-time video.

**Snack:** His choice of the month's ocean snacks — he helps make it, counting and sorting ingredients as the final math activity.

**Celebrate the process, not just the products:**
- "I love how you explained your ideas."
- "You thought carefully about where each animal lives."
- "You noticed so many details."
- "Your curiosity made this whole month amazing."
