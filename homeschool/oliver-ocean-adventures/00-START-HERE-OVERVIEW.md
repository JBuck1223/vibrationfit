# Oliver's Ocean Adventures — Month at a Glance

**Created for:** Oliver (Age 6, 1st Grade)
**Theme:** Ocean Adventures
**Style:** Play-based, hands-on, interest-led, child-centered — at a true 1st grade level

## How to Use This Folder

The curriculum is broken into **4 weekly lesson plans**, each with a suggested daily schedule (Monday–Friday). Week 1 is a placement week — it finds Oliver's actual level in reading and math, and every week includes "Stepping Down / Stepping Up" adjustments so the plan meets him where he is.

| File | Week | Focus |
|------|------|-------|
| `01-WEEK-1.md` | Week 1 | Placement week — short vowels & CVC words, counting ceiling, Wonder Wall, Drawing #1 |
| `02-WEEK-2.md` | Week 2 | Ocean Animals — digraphs (sh/ch/th/wh), addition & subtraction facts, Make-Ten Go Fish |

| `04-WEEK-4.md` | Week 4 | Putting It Together — vowel teams & compound words, time, money, polygons, celebration |
| `05-BOOKS-AND-MEDIA.md` | All | Age-appropriate book list, videos, and decodable readers |

**You do not need to do everything.** Follow Oliver's interest. If he wants three days on sharks, take them — the daily schedules are a rhythm, not a requirement.

## The Month's Two Spines

**Phonics ladder (literacy):** short vowels & CVC → digraphs sh/ch/th/wh → silent e → vowel teams & compound words. Week 1's placement check finds his rung; each week climbs one. Plus 5 sight words a week and 10 minutes of real reading daily.

**Math ladder:** counting within 120 & comparing numbers → addition/subtraction facts to 20 → place value & two-digit math → time, money, and geometry. Every day includes a story problem with objects — no worksheets required.

## The Daily Rhythm (about 2.5–3 hours, spread through the day)

| Block | Time | What Happens |
|-------|------|--------------|
| Morning Circle | 10–15 min | **He reads the calendar and writes the date**, yesterday/today/tomorrow, counting warm-up (count on, skip count) |
| Phonics & Reading | 25–30 min | The week's phonics pattern (games, word building, dictation) + **10 min reading decodables at his level** |
| Writing | 10–15 min | One or more journal sentences he writes himself — capital, spaces, period, invented spelling welcome |
| Math Through Play | 20–25 min | The day's math focus with objects + one ocean story problem |
| Ocean Science / Inquiry | 20–30 min | Books, experiments, research, Wonder Wall questions |
| Movement Break | 10 min | Ocean animal dances (links in the weekly plans) |
| Creative Time | 20–30 min | Art, crafts, building — connected to the theme |
| Story Time | 15–20 min | Read-aloud; he finds title/author/illustrator and retells first/next/last |
| Snack | — | Ocean snack doubles as a math problem ("eat 4 of your 12 blueberries — how many left?") |

## Monthly Learning Goals

By the end of this unit, Oliver will:

**Literacy**
- Read and write CVC words, digraph words (sh/ch/th/wh), silent-e words, and vowel-team words (ee/ea/ai/ay)
- Read ~15 sight words; read decodable books daily with growing fluency
- Write sentences independently (capital, spaces, end punctuation), building to 3-sentence pieces
- Build and split compound words; clap syllables

**Math**
- Count within 120 — forward, backward, and from any starting number; skip count by 2s, 5s, 10s
- Add and subtract within 20, including missing-number problems and fact families
- Understand tens and ones; add one-digit numbers to two-digit numbers with objects
- Tell time to the hour and half-hour (analog + digital); sort AM/PM
- Identify coins and pay exact amounts
- Name polygons (pentagon, hexagon, octagon, rhombus, trapezoid) and count sides/vertices
- Collect and interpret data with tally charts

**Science**
- Ask questions, predict, investigate, and record like a scientist (the Wonder Wall cycle)
- Learn ocean habitats and what living things need; sink/float and salt-water experiments

**Creative Arts, SEL & Gross Motor**
- Ocean art and recycled-material building; drawing observations
- Confidence sharing ideas, presenting to the family, handling winning/losing
- Ocean animal movement, balance, coordination

## Materials to Gather Before Week 1

**Literacy**
- Whiteboard and markers; magnetic letters or letter tiles
- **Decodable readers** (BOB Books sets 1–3, or free printables from progressivephonics.com) — the single most important purchase
- Index cards for word cards, sight words, and compound-word puzzles
- A journal/notebook for daily sentences + the Scientist Journal

**Math**
- Plastic ocean animals, shells, rocks, blue buttons (need 40–60 countable objects)
- Small cups (for Shell Bank cups of ten); a 120 chart (free printable)
- Goldfish crackers; deck of cards for Go Fish
- A play analog clock (or paper-plate clock you make together); real coins
- Pattern blocks or paper shapes; a ruler with inches and centimeters
- A wall calendar he can reach and write on

**Science & Art**
- Paper, crayons, paint, glue; recycled materials bin
- Clear bottles/glasses, salt, food coloring, oil (experiments); a hand magnifier

**Display**
- Wall space for the **Wonder Wall** (two columns: "What Oliver Knows" / "What Oliver Is Wondering") — up in Week 1, stays all month
- A special spot for his artwork

## Guiding Principles (keep these on the fridge)

1. **Meet him at his rung.** Week 1's placement is the truth; the plan bends to it, not the other way around.
2. **Ask, don't tell.** "What do you notice?" beats "This is a coral reef."
3. **He writes; you don't scribe what he can spell out himself.** Invented spelling is how first graders learn to write.
4. **There are no wrong answers** in brainstorms and predictions.
5. **Praise the thinking, not the product.** "You noticed so many details."
6. **Ten minutes daily beats an hour weekly** — especially for reading and math facts.
