# Oliver's Ocean Adventures — Month at a Glance

**Created for:** Oliver (Age 6)
**Theme:** Ocean Adventures
**Style:** Play-based, hands-on, interest-led, child-centered

---

## How to Use This Folder

The original curriculum has been broken into **4 weekly lesson plans**, each with a suggested daily schedule (Monday–Friday). Each week builds on the last:

| File | Week | Focus |
|------|------|-------|
| `01-WEEK-1.md` | Week 1 | "What Do I Know?" — Letter O, assessment, brainstorm, first ocean drawing |
| `02-WEEK-2.md` | Week 2 | Ocean Animals — Letter C, counting & grouping, Go Fish |
| `03-WEEK-3.md` | Week 3 | Young Researcher — Letters E & A, patterns, research week, Mote visit |
| `04-WEEK-4.md` | Week 4 | Putting It Together — Letter N, building OCEAN, second drawing, celebration |
| `05-BOOKS-AND-MEDIA.md` | All | Age-appropriate book list, videos, and songs by week |
| `06-PROGRESS-TRACKER.md` | All | Simple checklists to track letters, goals, and milestones |

**You do not need to do everything.** Follow Oliver's interest. If he wants to spend three days on sharks, let him. The daily schedules are a rhythm, not a requirement.

## The Daily Rhythm (about 2–2.5 hours of "school," spread through the day)

| Block | Time | What Happens |
|-------|------|--------------|
| Morning Circle | 10–15 min | Song, calendar, review the week's letter and vocabulary words |
| Letter Time | 15–20 min | Multi-sensory letter practice (Play-Doh, sand, tracing, punch pages) |
| Math Through Play | 15–20 min | Sorting, counting, patterns — always with objects, never just worksheets |
| Ocean Science / Inquiry | 20–30 min | Books, videos, experiments, Wonder Wall questions |
| Movement Break | 10 min | Ocean animal dances (links in each week's plan) |
| Creative Time | 20–30 min | Art, crafts, building — connected to the week's theme |
| Story Time | 15–20 min | Read-aloud from the book list (or story-time videos) |
| Snack | — | Ocean-themed snack doubles as a counting/sorting activity |

**Play-Doh letters happen every day** — this is non-negotiable in the original plan. It builds fine motor skills, eye-hand coordination, and letter knowledge all at once. Keep visual letter cards nearby whenever he builds.

## Monthly Learning Goals (from the curriculum)

By the end of this unit, Oliver will:

**Literacy**
- Recognize uppercase and lowercase letters (focus: O, C, E, A, N)
- Strengthen letter sounds and early phonics
- Begin writing familiar words independently (goal word: OCEAN)
- Build confidence printing letters

**Science**
- Learn about the ocean and ocean animals
- Develop prediction and observation skills
- Record discoveries like a scientist

**Math**
- Count objects (to 10 forward and backward)
- Sort and classify; compare sizes
- Practice patterns (AB, ABC, and his own)
- Measure with non-standard materials

**Creative Arts**
- Ocean-inspired art, building with recycled materials, drawing observations

**Social-Emotional**
- Confidence sharing ideas, asking questions, seeing many ways to solve problems

**Gross Motor**
- Dance and move like ocean animals, balance, coordination, movement games

## Materials to Gather Before Week 1

**Literacy**
- Whiteboard and markers
- Trays of sand, sugar, and flour (for letter tracing)
- Play-Doh (daily letter building)
- Magnetic letters, letter tiles, or cardboard letters you cut together
- Printed letter tracing pages (several copies — Evelyn can use them too)
- Thumbtack + cardboard/cork board/thick newspaper stack (for punch-tracing)

**Math**
- Plastic ocean animals (a tube of sea creatures from any dollar store works)
- Shells, rocks, blue buttons, toy fish
- Goldfish crackers (patterns, counting, and snack)
- Deck of Go Fish cards (or a standard deck — Schylling "Gone Fish'n" is the linked option)

**Art & Science**
- Paper, crayons, paint, glue
- Recycled materials (boxes, tubes, bottle caps) for building
- A notebook or folder to be Oliver's "Scientist Journal" — he records predictions and discoveries here all month

**Display**
- Wall space for the **Wonder Wall** (two columns: "What Oliver Knows" / "What Oliver Is Wondering") — this goes up in Week 1 and stays up all month
- A special spot to display his artwork

## Community Connection: Mote Marine Laboratory & Aquarium

- Mote offers **homeschool programs Tuesday afternoons at 1:30** designed for kids Oliver's age — hands-on marine science with real educators.
- The suggested visit is planned for **Week 3** (research week), but any Tuesday works. Book ahead.
- Before the visit, help Oliver pick 1–2 questions from his Wonder Wall to ask an educator.

## Guiding Principles (keep these on the fridge)

1. **Ask, don't tell.** "What do you notice?" beats "This is a coral reef."
2. **Record his words exactly** — on the back of art, in the journal. It shows his ideas matter and links speech to writing.
3. **There are no wrong answers** during brainstorms and predictions.
4. **Praise the thinking, not the product.** "You noticed so many details" instead of "Good job."
5. **Predictions are not about being right** — they're about thinking before discovering.
