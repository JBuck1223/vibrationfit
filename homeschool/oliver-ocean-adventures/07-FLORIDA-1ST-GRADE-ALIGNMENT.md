## Part 1: What Florida Actually Requires of Homeschoolers

Good news first: under Florida's home education statute (s. 1002.41, F.S.), **you are NOT legally required to follow the state standards, use specific curriculum, or cover specific subjects.** The law requires exactly three things:

1. **Notice of Intent** — filed with your county school district superintendent within 30 days of starting (child's name, address, date of birth). One-time, until you terminate.
2. **Portfolio of records** — a log of educational activities kept *as you go* (not reconstructed later), the **titles of reading materials used**, and **samples of writings, worksheets, and creative materials**. Keep it for 2 years; the district can inspect with 15 days' written notice (they rarely do).
3. **Annual evaluation** — filed each year on the anniversary of your Notice of Intent, showing progress "commensurate with his ability." Most families use a portfolio review by any Florida-certified teacher (~$50–60); other options are a nationally normed test, the state assessment, or a licensed psychologist.

**This curriculum produces the portfolio automatically:**

| Portfolio Requirement | Where It Comes From |
|---|---|
| Contemporaneous activity log | Weekly checklists + Friday reflection notes in `06-PROGRESS-TRACKER.md` (date them as you go) |
| Titles of reading materials | `05-BOOKS-AND-MEDIA.md` — circle/date each book as it's read |
| Writing samples | Daily journal sentences, Drawing #1 and #2 with his sentences, experiment write-ups, Mote journal pages |
| Creative materials | Photos of crafts, word builds, Shell Bank, tally charts, the Wonder Wall |

**One habit:** photograph anything 3-D (crafts, shell counts, pattern-block fish) — dated photos count as portfolio samples.

The evaluation standard is progress **commensurate with ability**, not grade-level benchmarks. Week 1's placement baseline plus Week 4's re-assessment, recorded side by side in the tracker, is exactly the evidence an evaluator wants to see.

## Part 3: Remaining Gaps — Spread Over Future Months

With the upgraded plans, this unit now touches most Grade 1 benchmarks. What's left for months 2+:

**Literacy**
- **R-controlled vowels (er, ir, ur, ar, or)** — the natural next rung (tuRtle, shaRk, wateR); then oo, ou/ow, au/aw, oi/oy
- **Word endings** -ing, -ed, -est, -s/-es; **open vs. closed syllables** (seeded with "o-cean" this month)
- **Opinion writing** (ELA.1.C.1.3): "My favorite ocean animal is ___ because ___" — easy add any month
- Growing the sight-word bank toward ~200 high-frequency words by year's end

**Math**
- **Fractions (MA.1.FR.1.1):** halves and fourths of circles/rectangles, explicitly — pairs beautifully with telling time to the half-hour
- **Two-digit addition/subtraction** beyond exploration; adding tens to any number fluently
- **Fact automaticity to 10** — keep a daily 5-minute game going every month (this is a year-long build)
- Pictographs (they've done tally charts; pictographs are one step further)

**Science (one Big Idea per future month)**
- SC.1.E.5.1–5.4 — stars, gravity, magnifiers, sun (a Space month covers all four)
- SC.1.E.6.1, 6.3 — Earth's surface; fast vs. slow changes (beach erosion = ocean tie-in)
- SC.1.P.12.1 / 13.1 — motion; push and pull (waves push and pull!)
- SC.1.L.14.2 — plant parts (grow something; seaweed vs. land plants)
- SC.1.L.16.1 — offspring resemble parents (baby animal names: pup, calf, fry)

**Social Studies (not yet covered — start small)**
- **Geography:** globe/map work — the 5 oceans, Florida, the Gulf; make a treasure map with a map key
- **Civics/Community:** marine scientists and lifeguards as community helpers; why aquariums have rules
- **Economics:** the Gift Shop game already covers goods/buyers/sellers — name the vocabulary

## Part 4: The Ask for the Curriculum Writer

Send monthly along with the tracker notes. Each future unit should include:

1. **The next phonics rung** with word lists, daily dictation, and decodable reading — not letter-of-the-week. Target: reading full easy readers by spring.
2. **A math spine**: daily fact practice, counting range extension, place value work, plus ONE of time/money/fractions/geometry as the month's focus skill.
3. **Daily writing** with a monthly genre focus (narrative → expository → opinion).
4. **One science Big Idea** from the remaining list above.
5. **A social studies thread** (maps, community, economics) themed to the month.
6. **A placement-style checklist** (like `06-PROGRESS-TRACKER.md`) so the portfolio and annual evaluation stay effortless.
7. **Step-down and step-up options** for every week, so the plan flexes to where Oliver actually is.
